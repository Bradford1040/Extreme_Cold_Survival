## (2) The Build: Survival-Grade Gooseneck Engineering 🚛🏗️

**The Philosophy:** Commercial RVs are "toys." This is a **Gooseneck Survival Pod** designed for an F-350 and the brutal reality of the Dalton Highway.

### 1. The Chassis (Welder’s Strategy) ⚡

- **Material:** Strictly **ASTM A500 Grade C** low-temp steel for the hitch and flex points to prevent cold-weather brittleness.
- **The "Torque Tube":** A large-diameter center pipe welded into the frame to stop "corkscrewing" on uneven trails.
- **Gooseneck Advantage:** Putting the weight directly over the F-350 axle for zero sway and maximum traction in the mud.

### 2. The Living Pod (Carpenter’s Strategy) 🪚

- **The "Arctic" Shell:** Moving beyond RV standards to an **R-21 to R-30 insulation rating** using closed-cell spray foam (acting as a vapor barrier) and triple-pane Arctic windows.
- **Layout:** \* **Master Suite:** Utilizing the gooseneck overhang for a dedicated "upstairs" bedroom.

    - **The Dog Den:** A custom-built, climate-controlled "den" under the bedroom for Moose and Vash.
- **Moisture Control:** Built-in rain-screen gaps behind interior walls to prevent condensation rot—critical when living with large dogs in the cold.

### 3. Survival Systems ❄️

- **The Heated "Basement":** A custom-welded aluminum pan under the floor. Diesel heater ducting runs *through* this space to keep water lines and the **100-gallon fuel cell** at 50°F when it’s $-30$°F outside.
- **Dual-Heat Source:** A primary **Toyotomi/Webasto** diesel heater for efficiency, backed up by a tiny **Cubic Mini wood stove** to dry out the air.
- **Power:** Vertical-mounted solar racks (to catch the low Alaskan sun) and an insulated, internal battery box for the LiFePO4 cells.
